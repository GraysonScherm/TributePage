Otto von Bismarck

The first chancellor of Germany

Otto von Bismarck pictured in armor.

Brief History:

  • 1815 - Born in Schönhausen, Germany
  • 1832 - Began to study law at the University of Göttingen, Germany, where he remained for a year.
    Next year, he enrolled at the University of Berlin.
  • 1847 - He served as a delegate in the new Prussian parliament, the Vereinigter Landtag. Here he
    earned a name for himself as a royalist, who spoke against the liberal, anti-autocratic Revolutions.
  • 1851 - He was appointed as a Prussian envoy at the Diet of the German Confederation in Frankfurt.
    He was later appointed into the Prussian House of Lords, for which he gave up his seat at the Landtag.
  • 1861 - After William I became the King of Prussia, he was chosen to serve as Minister-President and
    Foreign Minister the following year. He exhibited great diplomatic skills in the position.
  • 1864 - In order to built Prussian power in Europe, he launched a series of wars. He attacked
    Denmark and took control of the German-speaking territories of Schleswig-Holstein.

"The great questions of the day will not be settled by means of speeches and majority decisions but by iron and blood."

--Otto von Bismarck
